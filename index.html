The Problem

Long Work Hours Shouldn't Mean Daily Pain

Millions of IT professionals silently suffer from preventable musculoskeletal conditions caused by their work environment.

🦟

Chronic Neck Pain

Forward head posture from screen work adds up to 60 lbs of pressure on your cervical spine. Left untreated, it becomes chronic.

🦴

Back Pain & Disc Issues

Sitting 8+ hours without proper lumbar support strains your spinal discs and muscles — leading cause of work absenteeism globally.

👁️

Digital Eye Strain

Staring at screens for hours causes eye fatigue, dry eyes, blurred vision, and headaches — affecting 75% of computer users.

🪑

Poor Sitting Posture

Without ergonomic awareness, most people adopt slouched postures that compress vital structures and lead to long-term musculoskeletal damage.

⏰

Prolonged Static Postures

Remaining in one position for hours reduces circulation, increases muscle fatigue, and leads to progressive stiffness and joint degeneration.

😔

Fatigue & Burnout

Physical discomfort directly reduces cognitive performance and emotional wellbeing — poor ergonomics isn't just a body problem, it's a productivity crisis.

Personalised Solutions

Tailored Ergonomic Recommendations

Every recommendation is grounded in occupational therapy practice and adapted to your specific risk profile.

Neck Pain

Cervical Spine Support

Position monitor so the top third of the screen sits at eye level
Perform chin tuck exercises every 30 minutes to reset cervical alignment
Keep neck in neutral — avoid sustained forward head posture
Use a document holder to avoid repetitive downward head movement
Set keyboard and mouse at elbow height to reduce shoulder elevation
Back Pain

Lumbar Spine Support

Maintain natural lumbar curve — use a rolled towel if your chair lacks support
Set hips slightly higher than knees to reduce disc compression
Stand up and walk for 2–3 minutes every 45 minutes of sitting
Perform seated hip flexor stretches and lumbar extensions regularly
Engage core muscles while seated to provide dynamic spinal stability
Eye Strain

Visual Comfort & Relief

Follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 min, look 20 ft away for 20 seconds
Blink consciously and frequently to prevent dry eye syndrome
Set screen brightness to match ambient room light levels
Position screen 50–70 cm from eyes — arm's length as a starting guide
Use warm-toned lighting to reduce harsh contrast between screen and room
The OT Difference

Why OT Professionals Understand Workplace Pain Better

OT is not just rehabilitation — it's about helping people function better in everyday life, including how they work.

Occupational Therapy (OT) is a healthcare profession focused on helping people perform meaningful daily activities — or "occupations" — with greater ease, comfort, and safety. For most working adults, their job is one of their most important daily occupations.

OT professionals are trained to look beyond symptoms. They assess how a person moves, sits, reaches, and interacts with their environment — and identify what changes can make that interaction healthier and more sustainable.

Ergonomics is a natural fit for OT. It's not only about chairs and desks — it's about how people interact with their work environment and how workplace habits affect health, comfort, productivity, and long-term wellbeing.
